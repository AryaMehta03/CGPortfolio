Summary

The plant matches cycle to resource: use flash steam for hotter brine (T > ~180 °C) and binary/ORC for moderate brine (~120–180 °C) to maximize net MW. Closed-loop reinjection sustains reservoir pressure and chemistry, while H₂S/silica control and fit-for-climate ACC/cooling tower protect assets. Grid interface uses standard step-up + switchyard with EMS/protective relays.

Why this architecture?

Rationale

The plant selects the conversion path to fit the reservoir: flash steam if the brine is hot enough to separate usable steam, or binary/ORC if moderate, using a low-boiling working fluid to extract heat efficiently. Closed reinjection maintains pressure and limits surface discharge, extending field life. H₂S/silica control, scaling inhibitors, and climate-appropriate ACC/cooling tower maintain efficiency and protect metallurgy. With a step-up transformer + switchyard and EMS/relays, the plant is grid-ready, fault-tolerant, and provides 24×7 low-carbon baseload with a small footprint.

Explainer

Explainer

Source of energy

  • Liquid-dominated geothermal reservoir delivers hot brine via production wells.
  • Reservoir temperature/chemistry determine the best conversion path.

Conversion process

  • Flash path (hotter fields): Brine → flash separator → steam turbine → generator.
  • Binary/ORC path (moderate fields): Brine → heat exchanger → low-boiling working fluid → ORC turbine → generator.
  • Condenser (ACC/cooling tower) rejects heat; cooled brine → re-injection wells.
  • Chemistry control: H₂S abatement, silica/scale inhibitors to protect equipment.

Output / utilization

  • Continuous baseload electricity with high capacity factor.
  • Optional co-located low-grade heat for drying/space heat where viable.

Real-world relevance

  • 24×7 low-carbon power; modest land/water use when air-cooled.
  • Reinjection sustains reservoir integrity and regulatory compliance.
  • Choosing flash vs ORC by measured temperature/chemistry minimizes LCOE and maximizes uptime.

Quick Specs

Specs
  • Cycle: Flash (>~180 °C) or Binary/ORC (~120–180 °C)
  • Heat rejection: ACC or cooling tower (climate-based)
  • Reservoir: Closed-loop reinjection, chemistry management
  • Grid: Step-up transformer, switchyard, EMS/relays
