Biogas System for a 100-Cattle Dairy Farm

Anahita Shrestha | Clean and Green Energy Portfolio

Summary

This design converts dairy manure into clean energy using mesophilic anaerobic digestion (~35 °C, 30-40 days HRT), delivering biogas for electricity/heat and digestate for soil health. The architecture prioritizes gas quality, safe operation, and modular utilization paths (CHP, direct heat, Bio-CNG).

Why this architecture?

Rationale

Cattle dung is wet and consistent, making it ideal for mesophilic anaerobic digestion (~35 °C, 30-40 d HRT) with steady CH₄ yield and simple O&M. The gas train-moisture trap + H₂S scrubberprotects burners/CHP from corrosion and deposits, while a low-pressure gas holder buffers slow biological production against peaky farm demand. The layout intentionally exposes three utilization paths—CHP (power + hot water), direct heat, and Bio-CNG upgradingso energy can be matched to evolving loads without redesign. Downstream, solid-liquid separation closes the nutrient loop with compost and liquid bio-fertilizer, reducing synthetic NPK. Finally, embedded safety interlocks (PRV, flame arrestor, leak detection, earthing) keep operations compliant and robust.

Explainer (150-200 words)

Explainer

Source of energy

  • 100 cattle → ~1,000-1,500 kg/day manure with high biodegradable organics.
  • Moisture content and stability suit wet anaerobic digestion.

Conversion process

  • Manure diluted (~1:1) and fed into a mesophilic digester (~35 °C).
  • Microbial stages convert organics → biogas (≈55-65% CH₄).
  • Gas conditioning: moisture trap → H₂S scrubber → meter.
  • Digestate → solid-liquid separator for compost and liquid bio-fertilizer.

Output / utilization

  • Typical yield: ~30-52 m³ biogas/day (site dependent).
  • CHP genset: ~30% electrical + useful heat for cleaning/CIP.
  • Direct burners: cooking, process heat (e.g., milk-chiller burner).
  • Upgrading: Bio-CNG for vehicles or cylinder storage.
  • Digestate: replaces synthetic NPK; improves soil structure/CEC.

Real-world relevance

  • Reduces LPG/diesel OPEX, odors, and pathogens; lowers methane emissions.
  • Creates revenue/savings via power/heat/fertilizer.
  • Simple, scalable equipment with PRV, flame arrestor, leak detection for safety.

Quick Specs

Specs
  • Process: Mesophilic AD (~35 °C), HRT 30-40 days
  • Gas quality: 55-65% CH₄ after moisture/H₂S removal
  • Safety: PRV, flame arrestor, LEL detector, earthing
  • Outputs: Electricity/heat (CHP), direct heat, Bio-CNG; compost + liquid fertilizer
